What is AI?

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is when computers can do smart things that usually need human brains, like recognizing faces, understanding speech, or playing games.

AI learns from lots of examples (called data) to find patterns, just like how you learn to recognize cats after seeing many pictures of them. Once it knows the patterns, it can make predictions about new things it hasn't seen before.

AI in Your Daily Life

You probably use AI dozens of times a day without even realizing it. Here are three examples you'll recognize.

🎬

YouTube Recommendations

AI analyzes what you watch, like, and skip to predict what you'll enjoy next. It compares your habits with millions of other viewers to serve up videos that keep you watching.

🔓

Face Unlock

Your phone creates a 3D map of your face using thousands of invisible dots. Each time you glance at it, AI matches your live face to that map in milliseconds to unlock securely.

🦜

Duolingo Adapts to You

AI tracks which words you get right or wrong and how fast you answer. It adjusts lesson difficulty in real time, giving you more practice on weak spots while skipping what you've mastered.
